Electrical Substation Basics

Electrical Substation Basics (Canada – Typical Medium Station)

An electrical substation is a facility where electrical power is transformed, switched, and distributed to different parts of the electrical grid. Substations reduce high transmission voltage to safer distribution voltage used by cities, industries, and residential areas.

Main Functions of a Substation

  • Step down transmission voltage to distribution voltage
  • Switch power lines on or off
  • Protect electrical equipment from faults
  • Monitor power flow and system conditions
  • Control and isolate sections of the grid

Typical Voltage Levels in Canada

System Level Typical Voltage Purpose
Transmission 115 kV – 500 kV Long distance power transport
Subtransmission 44 kV – 115 kV Regional distribution
Distribution 4 kV – 27.6 kV Supply to communities and industry

Main Components of a Substation

1. Power Transformer

  • Steps voltage up or down
  • Major and most expensive equipment
  • Oil-filled and heavily protected

2. Circuit Breakers

  • Interrupt current during faults
  • Protect equipment and lines
  • Can be SF6, vacuum, or oil type

3. Disconnect Switches

  • Provide visible isolation
  • Used during maintenance
  • Not designed to interrupt load current

4. Busbars

  • Metal conductors that distribute power inside the station
  • Connect transformers, breakers, and feeders

5. Protection Relays

  • Detect faults such as short circuits
  • Send trip signals to circuit breakers

6. Instrument Transformers

  • Current Transformers (CT)
  • Voltage Transformers (PT / VT)
  • Provide safe measurement signals

Control and Protection System

Modern substations use digital control systems including SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition). Operators can monitor voltage, current, and breaker status from a control center.

  • SCADA system
  • Protection relays
  • Control panels
  • Communication systems

Safety Systems

  • Grounding grid
  • Lightning arresters
  • Fire protection systems
  • Arc flash protection

Typical Layout

A medium-sized Canadian substation normally includes:

  • Incoming high-voltage transmission line
  • Main power transformer
  • Switchgear and breakers
  • Protection relay panels
  • Outgoing distribution feeders

Common Tests During Commissioning

  • Transformer insulation resistance test
  • Circuit breaker timing test
  • Relay protection testing
  • Ground grid resistance test
  • Functional SCADA testing
