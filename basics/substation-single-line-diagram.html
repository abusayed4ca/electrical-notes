Substation Single Line Diagram (SLD) Explained

A Single Line Diagram (SLD) is a simplified drawing used in electrical engineering to represent the power system of a substation. Instead of showing every wire, a single line is used to represent three-phase electrical power circuits.

Why Engineers Use Single Line Diagrams

  • Understand system layout quickly
  • Analyze electrical faults
  • Design protection systems
  • Plan commissioning tests
  • Troubleshoot electrical problems

Typical Substation Power Flow

In a typical substation, power flows through equipment in the following order:

  • Incoming Transmission Line
  • Circuit Breaker
  • Disconnect Switch
  • Current / Voltage Transformers
  • Power Transformer
  • Busbar System
  • Outgoing Feeders

Common Symbols in Single Line Diagrams

Symbol Equipment Purpose
CB Circuit Breaker Interrupts current during faults
DS Disconnect Switch Provides isolation during maintenance
CT Current Transformer Measures current safely
VT / PT Voltage Transformer Measures system voltage
T1 Power Transformer Steps voltage up or down
BUS Busbar Main electrical distribution point

Types of Substation Bus Configurations

  • Single Bus
  • Main and Transfer Bus
  • Double Bus Double Breaker
  • Ring Bus
  • Breaker-and-a-Half Scheme

Large transmission substations often use more complex bus schemes to improve reliability and allow maintenance without power interruption.

How SLDs Are Used During Commissioning

  • Verify correct equipment connections
  • Identify testing points
  • Check protection relay wiring
  • Confirm breaker control circuits
  • Validate power flow paths

Field Tip

When working in substations, always keep a copy of the Single Line Diagram. It helps quickly understand how power flows and which breaker or disconnect affects each part of the system.
